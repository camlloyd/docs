Definition

What I Mean by Agentic Genomics

  • An AI agent that can discover, configure, execute, and chain bioinformatics operations from natural-language input.
  • Not chat-with-your-data. Not RAG over papers. Tool use with consequence.
  • The shift: orchestration is becoming cheap. Validation is now the binding constraint.
  • The most dangerous errors look plausible. Plausible-but-wrong scales faster than wrong-and-obvious.

Corpas, Fatumo, Guio. Agentic genomics: from pipeline automation to autonomous validation. bioRxiv 2026.

Three failure modes that matter clinically: stochastic variation across runs, confabulation of population-specific data, and silently wrong outputs that look right. I'll show you data on all three. TIMING: 1.5 min

Empirical question

Can a SKILL.md Produce Clinical-Grade Behaviour?

  • Domain: pharmacogenomics. Genotype to phenotype to drug recommendation, with CPIC ground truth.
  • Stakes: real. DPYD rs3918290 T/T + standard fluorouracil = potentially lethal.
  • Design: 9 frontier LLMs × 12 PGx cases × 3 populations × 2 conditions × 3 runs.
  • Conditions: with vs without ClawBio SKILL.md specification.
  • Populations: European, admixed Latin American (Peruvian Genome Project), East African (Uganda Genome Resource).

1,944 evaluations. Submitted to Briefings in Bioinformatics (Corpas, Iacoangeli, Fatumo, Guio).

If a specification cannot improve reliability in pharmacogenomics, where the guideline is fixed and the consequences are clinically measurable, it's not going to help in less structured domains. So this is a stress test of the skill-library hypothesis. TIMING: 2 min

Without specification, frontier LLMs are less reliable on safety-critical cases when the patient context diverges from European training data. The specification carries curated population-specific data (S. Fatumo for East African, H. Guio for Latin American), closing the gap entirely.

This is why open infrastructure matters. The model has European data baked in. Anyone working on non-European populations needs to inject domain expertise into the contract. That's a structural argument for open, community-curated skills, not a model-vendor product. TIMING: 2 min
Interpretation

Skills as Auditable Contracts, Not Prompt Engineering

What the data says

  • Stochastic variation: eliminated
  • Population-dependent error: eliminated
  • DPYD lethal-case errors: eliminated
  • Trade-off: contextual narrative drops (B1 0.69 to 0.38). Acceptable for clinical-grade.

Why this matters structurally

  • SKILL.md = regulatory-auditable specification
  • Git version = chain-of-custody for clinical use
  • Determinism = prerequisite for multi-site validation
  • Open library = community curation of population data
The objection is "you just gave the model the answer". That's the point. Domain expertise is the contract. The model is the executor. Software engineering separates spec from implementation; clinical AI should too. TIMING: 2 min
Practitioner view

Real Data: Same Family, 18 Different CAD PRS

  • Five 30x WGS family genomes (mine, my wife's, three children). Released openly on Zenodo.
  • Asked the agent: "Estimate cardiovascular risk from these genotypes."
  • Without skill constraint: 18 different PRS choices, contradictory rankings across reruns.
  • With ClawBio fine-mapping skill + reference panels: consistent ranking, traceable to specific PGS catalogue IDs.
  • Same input. Same agent. The contract is what changed.
The family demo is the everyday version of what the benchmark proves. The agent without contract is a sophisticated guesser. With the contract, it's an auditor of a declared analytical pipeline. TIMING: 1.5 min
Framework

Tiered Validation for Agentic Genomics

Research-grade

Unit tests, adversarial inputs, declared assumptions. Fit for hypothesis generation.

Benchmarked

Validated performance on reference datasets. Reproducibility across seeds and prompts. Most ClawBio skills sit here today.

Clinical-grade

External multi-site validation, regulatory alignment, near-zero false-negative tolerance. Empirically achievable on PGx, as shown today. Multi-site is the open work.

The benchmark gives us empirical evidence for two of the three clinical-grade properties (traceability, cross-population invariance). External multi-site validation is the next experiment.

I want to be careful not to overclaim. We have not demonstrated clinical deployment readiness. We have demonstrated a mechanism that addresses two of the three core requirements. The third needs cohort access we don't have. TIMING: 1.5 min

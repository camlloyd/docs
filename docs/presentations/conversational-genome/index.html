The founding idea

An operating system for personal genomics

By 2030 sequencing is commoditised. The bottleneck is no longer data generation; it is continuous understanding, trust, and interaction. Ten principles for the category we are building.

01

The category: a conversational genome

A conversational genome is a persistent, agentic interface to a person's genome that turns a static file into a lifelong, evidence-grounded dialogue. It is not a report you read once, not a chatbot bolted onto a PDF, and not a one-time clinical interpretation. 23andMe, Nebula, Sequencing.com, SOPHiA and Fabric all deliver an artefact: a result at a point in time. A conversational genome delivers a relationship over time, where every answer is executed by a versioned skill and re-derivable from first principles.

02

First principles: why reports are the wrong abstraction

A report freezes interpretation at the moment of sequencing, yet the genome is fixed for life while the science over it changes weekly. The static artefact cannot answer a question it did not anticipate, cannot update when ClinVar moves, and cannot express its own uncertainty. Conversation changes the abstraction from "deliver a document" to "answer any question, forever, with provenance," which is the only abstraction that survives decades of evolving evidence.

03

Architecture: agents over a trust layer

Natural language is mediated by specialised agents sitting on a non-negotiable trust layer.

Agent layer
Genome, Pharmacogenomics, Rare Disease, Ancestry, Family, Research, Clinical
Trust layer
Provenance, reproducibility, skill execution, confidence and abstention
Knowledge layer
ClinVar, PharmGKB, CPIC, OMIM, gnomAD, Ensembl, literature
Data layer
VCF, BAM/CRAM, polygenic scores, family pedigrees, EHR

Answers never originate in a model's free text. A question routes to a skill, the skill executes deterministically over the data and knowledge layers, and the result returns with its evidence and confidence. The demo above is this architecture, running.

04

ClawBio: the execution layer for conversational biology

ClawBio is the open-source framework that executes the skills. Conversational Genome is its flagship application, and the relationship is symbiotic: the application proves the framework at the bar clinical genomics demands, and the framework gives the application reproducibility for free. ClawBio stays open infrastructure so that skills become a shared standard, while the conversational layer is where the network effects compound, every new skill makes every genome more answerable.

05

Research agenda: where correctness must live

The first large-scale evidence is already in hand. Across 44,550 scored evaluations over nine frontier models and 110 CPIC cases, accuracy climbed as correctness was constrained, but only executing a versioned skill delivered the clinical-grade guarantees, and retrieval-augmenting a model with the correct guideline actually raised the lethal-class error rate. The five-year programme extends this into longitudinal reinterpretation, human-AI genomic interaction, multi-agent biomedical systems, and population-aware interpretation.

06

Equity as architecture, not compliance

The same unguarded pipeline that reaches high accuracy on European genomes degrades sharply on others. On real diplotypes it transferred to 72% in European, 51% in Latin American and 40% in East African individuals. Conversational Genome makes this a first-class output: every answer carries an ancestry-validity flag and exposes the evidence gap instead of hiding it. Quantified uncertainty across ancestries, built into the interface, is how equity becomes a differentiator and a global standard rather than a disclaimer.

07

Business model: one engine, many surfaces

The same execution engine serves consumers (a lifelong subscription to your own genome), researchers and biobanks (conversational cohorts at population scale), hospitals (point-of-care pharmacogenomics inside the EHR), pharma (trial enrichment and reinterpretation), and public health (equity-aware national programmes). Defensibility is not the model, which commoditises; it is the skill library, the provenance graph, and the trust that compounds as both grow.

08

Competitive analysis: the conversational wedge

Incumbent clinical platforms are strong at accredited single interpretations and weak at continuity and interaction. Consumer platforms are strong at scale and weak at rigour and trust. General AI assistants are fluent and dangerous, they are precisely the free-prompt condition our benchmark shows fails. The wedge is the combination no incumbent holds: conversational interaction with executed, auditable, ancestry-aware correctness.

09

Path to category leadership

Lead with the science, set the standard in the open, and let the community build the skills. Publish the benchmark and the architecture, keep ClawBio open so skills converge on a shared contract, define the provenance and reproducibility standard others adopt, and engage regulators early with auditability as the argument. The sequence that wins is evidence first, standard second, ecosystem third.

10

The 2035 vision

A patient asks their genome whether a new prescription is safe and gets a grounded answer before leaving the pharmacy. A clinician opens the conversational genome inside the EHR and sees the one pharmacogenomic flag that matters, with its evidence. A researcher interrogates a biobank by conversation instead of by query language. A regulator audits not a black box but a reproducible trace. The genome has stopped being a file you store and become a companion you consult, for life.
